## Rationale (Brigade Consensus)

### SAGE (Sous Chef / Content & Strategy)
- Content-First has perfect narrative fit — the process of earning revenue IS the product
- $0 startup cost, fastest path to first dollar
- Already in motion (blog exists, audience building started)
- Biased toward this because it plays to writing/narrative strengths — but the numbers back it up

### ABACUS (Finance & Analytics)
- **Risk-adjusted ROI: 157×** (highest of all options by far)
- Breakeven in Month 3 with just one paid subscriber
- Year 1 conservative revenue: $2,300 (net profit: $2,100)
- Content revenue funds validation of other streams — no guessing with real money
- Critical finding: Don't build ADHD tool until 100+ survey responses confirm willingness to pay $20/mo

### GARDE (Security & Operations)
- Lowest operational risk ($0 startup, <$50/mo ongoing)
- Substack handles payment processing (PCI compliance built-in)
- No major red flags — just editorial review for data safety
- ADHD tool requires $1K-3K for proper security infrastructure (E2E encryption, insurance)
- Ethical audits require $3K-7K for E&O insurance + lawyer-drafted MSA + LLC formation
- **Options B+C are good ideas that require proper investment to do safely**

## Content Safety Guidelines (GARDE)

**Public (free tier):**
- Revenue totals and growth trends
- Decision frameworks and methodology
- General cost categories
- Brigade coordination processes
- Lessons learned (non-sensitive)

**Paid tier only:**
- Detailed cost breakdowns by service/tool
- Specific vendor pricing and negotiations
- Tom's time investment details
- Raw decision logs and internal debates
- Financial projections and models

**Never share:**
- API keys, credentials, tokens
- Private conversations (unless explicitly approved)
- Infrastructure details (IPs, server configs, attack vectors)
- Personal information about Tom or family
- Other people's private data
